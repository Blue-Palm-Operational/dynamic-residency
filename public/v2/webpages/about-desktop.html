What Is Dynamic Residency?

Residence Is Never an Isolated Decision

Dynamic Residency is a specialised service platform focused on the economic and structural consequences of establishing residence in Türkiye. We do not treat residence as an isolated permit, a single tax rule or a destination choice. We examine the complete international situation in which the residence decision must operate.

That situation may include the country being left, current and future tax residence, foreign-source and Turkish-source income, companies and management activity, investments, property, family links, succession considerations and the practical steps required to implement a viable structure.

The objective is not to persuade every client to move to Türkiye. The objective is to determine whether Türkiye is suitable for the client's actual circumstances and, where it is suitable, to coordinate the route from analysis to implementation.

Why Dynamic Residency Exists

Close the Gap Between Information and a Decision That Works

International relocation is often explained through separate topics: residence permits, tax rates, property, company formation or lifestyle. In practice, the outcome depends on how those topics interact across more than one jurisdiction.

A decision that appears attractive in Türkiye may create an unintended result in the country being left. A foreign company may be affected by where it is managed. Income paid from abroad may not be foreign-source. Family ties, travel patterns or business activity may change the residence analysis. Timing may determine whether an opportunity remains available.

Dynamic Residency was created to close the gap between general information and a decision that can operate in real life. We begin while the important variables can still be managed — before residence changes, company management moves, assets are sold, ownership is restructured or family arrangements become difficult to reverse.

Türkiye's 20-Year Foreign Income Tax Exemption

Türkiye has introduced a potentially significant exemption for eligible individuals who become Turkish tax residents from 2026. The rule may be relevant to certain foreign-source income, but qualification depends on residence history, the true source of the income, the application process and the wider cross-border structure. It should therefore be reviewed as part of the complete residence decision, not as a stand-alone promise.
